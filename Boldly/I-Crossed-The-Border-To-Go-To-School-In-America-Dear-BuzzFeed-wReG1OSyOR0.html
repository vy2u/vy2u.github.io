I Crossed The Border To Go To School In America • Dear BuzzFeed

2016-01-25
one night my oldest brother and my parents decided we should learn English and that is a day their whole lives and future's changed dear BuzzFeed when I was five years old I lived in Tijuana Baja California Mexico with my older sister who was 8 my older brothers who were 11 and 20 my parents and my nanny one night my oldest brother and my parents decided we should learn English and that is a day their whole lives and futures changed every day for the next 10 years we cross the border to go to school in the u.s. at first it was all very exciting but we didn't really know what we were getting into we will wake up at around 3 a.m. so we could shower and get ready for school my mom dad would take turns to cross with us through the San Ysidro border we cross by car and would arrive at the border at around 4:30 a.m. to make line which could either be five minutes or four hours there was no way for us to know how long we'd be there sometimes fights would break out or officials would give us a difficult time forcing us to arrive very early in the morning if it only took us a short time to cross we would go to a park in front of my old elementary school and sleep for a while after about seven years we began crossing through the pedestrian lane alone after we crossed we would take a trolley than the bus that would drop us off in front of school this process would take about 40 minutes after 10 years of crossing the border we finally moved to the US the youngest of my brothers went on to graduate from San Diego State University as a computer science major and is working for a coding company my sister is a 4th year student at the University of California Berkeley my oldest brother owns an online bookstore that provides the US and Europe a massive amount of Latin American publications it's one of the biggest bibliographic catalogues of the Spanish language and finally I'm a student at the University of California Irvine as an undecided undeclared danger I wanted to tell you the story because we literally had a border to stop us from getting an education in the u.s. we faced so many challenges throughout the years fights breaking out patronizing cops and being late to school but we didn't let it stop us instead here we are 13 years later trying to make this country a better place thank you for taking the time to read this
