Dealing With Age Pressure | My Experience

2018-05-30
(light music) - Hey everyone, it's your girl Jenn and today, I wanted to film a really chill heart to heart about the topic of age pressure. It's something that I really struggled with and I know I'm not alone because every culture has societal expectations they put on us. Like, we need to be at a certain place when we reach a specific age. For example, there are some parts of the world where a woman is actually considered expired if they're not married by the age of 30 and that just bewilders me, it's insane and I'm so happy that I've partnered with SK2 to talk about this subject. So I recently saw SK2's video called The Expiry Date and I found it really moving because it really hit all the societal expectations and norms they put on women, whether it's finding a boyfriend on time, having a baby at a certain age and it made me really reflect on all of the times I felt pressured to make huge decisions in my life just because I reached a certain age. One of the earliest signs of age pressure was when I turned 18. 18 is the age where you're an official adult and you're supposed to be, you know, going to college, you're supposed to have moved out of your parents' place but I was in community college. I didn't get accepted to any of the schools I wanted to and so I felt like I was at this deficit trying to catch up with everybody and it was very panicky. I was able to find a creative outlet, which was creating YouTube videos so me and Sarah started our YouTube channel, Clothes Encounters, and it felt so good to have this release where we would just get together every weekend, film videos about our fashion and lifestyle and it was great and honestly, it was truly my light at that weird time in my life and so I just continued to swing both and then I transferred. I transferred to UC Davis and when I finally got in to that school, I felt this big release. I felt like I was finally on track. I was good for two years until it was about time to graduate. My parents started asking me, "Oh, like, Jenny, do you, like, are you gonna apply for jobs up there or down there, are you gonna stop doing your YouTube channel?" And I, honestly, didn't know whether I wanted to go the traditional route or the riskier route. I went with my gut because I just knew YouTube made me so happy and if I was gonna fail at doing something I loved, I would rather do that so I went with YouTube and, honestly, 22 to 24 was a very bright time in my life. I felt strong, I felt independent, I was living in Oakland and it just felt amazing. So fast forward to when I turned 25, I was back in Los Angeles and suddenly, I had my quote, unquote, quarter life crisis and it happened because my manager and my agent started to ask me more questions about where my channel was going or what I would turn my business into and I felt this pressure to figure out everything all over again and so the Virgo in me started to plan maniacally. I created this very extensive five year plan of all these goals I wanted to hit, like I wanted to buy a house, I wanted to have a retirement plan, I wanted to retire my parents, I wanted to have my channel at a certain place and it just felt like so much pressure and, for me, a lot of my pressures came from my career and for every woman it's different, like, I saw this Vanity Fair interview where they interviewed four amazing women in the digital space, including my girl, Aimee Song, and they all explained different pressures that they were faced with. So for some of them, it was to get married at a certain age, it was to have children at a certain age or to have their business sold at a certain age and so for every person it's different but those pressures are still there. I felt like I had to make it by the time I was 30 on YouTube because I was terrified that the older I got, the more irrelevant I would become on YouTube and when it was, like, 2015, 2016, there was this flux of all these young YouTubers and it was the first time where I was like, whoa, like, I am getting older. I just felt so insecure and unhappy with myself and my channel because I feel like my channel and myself, they really bleed in together and so if one is not doing well, then I'm not, and maybe on the outside, it seemed like everything was fine when I was 26 but it was a lot of instability and unhappiness with myself. Comparison is truly the thief of joy and I know with social media these days, it's so pervasive and everything's always in your face and you just can't help to compare your life with someone else's but that's when I knew I had to stop this delusion because everybody's journey is completely different. Just because one person has done this at a certain age doesn't mean that you have to. We're all on our own paths and I knew I had to change, I knew on my 27th birthday, I had to just get it together because it was not okay. I started to distance myself from my channel, like, it's still my baby and I love it but if my channel isn't doing hot, it doesn't affect me like it used to and I'm so glad that I'm taking care of myself better, mentally, and I'm in a lot, I'm in, like, such a great place right now. I am 27 years old and I am proud of where I am and I'm proud of where I'm going. I feel like every year of my 20's has been so radically different and I'm so proud of this evolution and I just know that comparing is a game where you will always lose. If I could go back to my younger self, I would tell her to be present and to enjoy life the way it exactly is then because it will never ever be like that ever again so enjoy it and stop focusing on where you constantly need to be because you're gonna be where you're gonna be. My age is only a tiny tiny facet of me, it does not define who I am and I absolutely do not have an expiry date until I actually am dead in the coffin, that is when I expire. Not when I start getting wrinkles, not if I don't hit a certain age deadline, I am here and I will live my life on my own terms. I would love for you guys to see SK2's I Never Expire video. I will leave a link in the description box and also the card so please, please go watch it, it is so inspiring and I hope it allows you to realize that you can live your life on your own schedule, not somebody else's. This is your life and only you can control it. I would love for the comments section to be just, like, a really supportive place where we can listen to each other and learn from each other. I wanna thank you guys so much for watching and I'll see you guys in the next one. Bye. Muah. (light music)
