And it just surprised me how many things are so easy to do in InDesign that are so hard to do in Photoshop or other programs. Simple things like making a paragraph line, a line that goes through the entire page. Literally, guys, the kind of lines I used for this first Artist of Life workbook was underline and that bottom... I literally just typed in a lot of underlines to make a line. But in InDesign, you could create lined pages with paragraph rules. And my favorite thing is called Paragraph Styling. That's where you can create a style, style this font or create some sort of font style, and you can apply it to, for example, all of your headings or all of your body text. And if later down the line– and this happens really often–you decide to change up your font style, you maybe want to make this header bigger or change it to a different font, you can change it and then Paragraph Styles will automatically change all of the other headings in the document so that you don't have to change them all one by one. You know what I mean? You make one change and it applies to all. Honestly, my point of being so long-winded is that it's always important to continue learning, continue honing your craft.
