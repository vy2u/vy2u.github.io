Minimalism: 3 Ways to Declutter Your Closet

2015-01-14
hi guys today I want to share three ways to declutter your closet for a more minimal 2015 first off let's clarify what minimalism means minimalism is not about not being able to have things it's actually about keeping only what adds value to your life everything you have should serve a purpose whether it's useful or it makes you happy if it doesn't then it's just clutter and minimalism is about clearing away the clutter to make room for what really matters because a clear space equals a clear mind alright now let's give your closet a new year makeover number one purge evaluate your closet and purge anything that you haven't worn in the past two years meaning if you haven't touched it since pre 2013 and chances are you're probably not going to wear it again ever if you have anything that's sentimental you can store those in a separate memory box so that it doesn't take valuable space in your closet number two try the reverse hanger trick I saw this on a blog and I'm trying it for the first time this year the idea is at the beginning of the year turn all of your hangers backwards and as you wear your clothes simply put them back the right way so that indicates that that piece of clothing is safe at the end of June do an evaluation of your closet see which pieces are still hung backwards and you can decide which ones you're ready to let go of and donate then continue the experiment until the end of the year and at the end of December that's when you do a honest evaluation of your entire closet look at the clothes that are still hung backwards at the end of December and just be honest with yourself you know if you haven't worn this piece in the entire year are you really going to wear it next year so this is a huge way to get rid of a big chunk of your closet and just start to declutter and minimalize number three create a capsule wardrobe this one is for the true minimal is a capsule wardrobe is a wardrobe that is made up of a set number of essential staple pieces usually created per season it's about selecting high quality pieces that you can mix and match and wear often that way you're more efficient with your clothes rather than constantly shopping for fast fashion trends that you wear only once or twice because you're only allowed a set number of pieces in your wardrobe you're forced to decide which pieces are the most valuable to you in your closet notice which Styles you love to wear the most often and then have those Styles make up the majority of your capsule it's helpful to create a color scheme and stick to a lot of neutral colors if you like you can add a couple of trendy pieces on top to keep your wardrobe updated each season for more info on creating a capsule wardrobe I've posted some really helpful
