Dear 7th Grade Me | A Poem

2014-07-27
it's actually a funny thing the older we get the more we care and the more we fret about how others perceive us and see us but not how we ourselves see ourselves I never was the most outgoing person and as I grew up my seclusion worsen I always chose to stay in and read which label Mia is quite an odd breed as others were attending social gatherings staying up till 3:00 singing and laughing I was losing myself in all these visions about projects and my future ambitions I always thought this wasn't a weakness that my creative mind was seen as uniqueness but that perception of myself was soon changed when I was called a nerd and seen a strange 7th grade me thought this was a tragedy as I overreacted and cried in agony but oh how I wish I could go back in claim that I was simply intelligent which was not a shame we live in a world dominated by entertainment where knowledge and wisdom are put in containment what one writes or what one reads has no value if it's not on a screen we can't endure a 10-minute conversation without our phones buzzing and vibration and no what a shame it would be if we left that phone as an absentee our talks become of surface level topics where the options of a conversation seemed to be microscopic we can't even involve ourselves in debates due to fear of offending our mates I thank God every day that 7th grade me refused to change I hope to never live in a place where scholars and inventors are seen as a disgrace on lines are such beautiful contraptions having the ability to form endless actions nothing compares to that feeling of pride when you solve an equation or create your own rhyme now that I'm older and slightly wiser I've given up the childish desire of wanting to fit in with these so-called friends when I have something far more valuable a brain and a lens you
